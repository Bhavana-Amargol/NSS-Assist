NATIONAL SERVICE SCHEME
About NSS

“NOT ME BUT YOU” SURANA COLLEGE,South End.Est: 1998

The National Service Scheme is into existence at the College since academic year 1998. Since the beginning, the college sub-unit works as per the regulations of The Bangalore University NSS unit. The volunteers of the college sub-unit have many special accolades to their credit.

Started initially in 37 universities involving 40,000 volunteers, the scheme has grown over the years and it is implemented today with an involvement of more than 2.6 million volunteers spread over in 200 universities, polytechnics and +2 systems. The efforts of NSS volunteers have been widely acclaimed by the community, universities, colleges and general public as the NSS volunteers have been rendering selfless service to the community.

NATIONAL SERVICE SCHEME

SYMBOL

The symbol for NSS has been based on the giant Rath wheel of the world famous Konark Sun Temple (The Black Pagoda) situated in Orissa, India. The wheel portrays the cycle of creation, preservation and release and signifies the movement in life across time and space, the symbol thus stands for continuity as well as change and implies the continuous striving of NSS for social change.

BADGE

The NSS symbol is embossed on the badge. The eight bars in the wheel represent the 24 hours of a day. The red colour indicates that the volunteer is full of young blood that is lively, active, energetic and full of high spirit. The navy-blue colour indicates the cosmos of which the NSS is tiny party, ready to.

MOTTO

The motto of NSS “Not Met But You”, reflects the essence of democratic living and upholds the need for self-less services. NSS helps the students develop appreciation to other person’s point of view and also show consideration to other living begins. The philosophy of the NSS is well doctrine in this motto, which underlines/ on the belief that the welfare of an individual is ultimately dependent on the welfare of the society on the whole and therefore, the NSS volunteers shall strive for the well-being of the society.

  • Understand themselves in relative to their community.
  • Identify the needs and problems of the community and involve them in problem.
  • Develop among themselves a sense of social and CIVIC responsibility.

PARADES

The participation of NSS Volunteers in Republic Day Parades started on an experimental basis initially in early 1990s has now become a regular feature. NSS contingents from the states/UTs participate in the Republic Day parade at New Delhi on 26th January every year. NSS contingents comprising of boys and girls will also participates in state level Republic Day Parade at Bangalore. As these are prestigious e of prevents, high degree of performance is expected from the participant volunteers. Therefore, very strict selection process is being resorted to select volunteers who meet the physical standards prescribed by the Ministry of Defence and who are physically strong to withstand rigorous training. The requisite physical standards are as follows:

BOYS
  1. Height: 165-175 ems
  2. Weight: Proportionate to height in both the cases.

GIRLS
  1. Height: 155-165 ems
  2. Weight: Proportionate to height in both the cases.
